**Functional Requirements (FRs)**These define the specific features and
capabilities the e-commerce website must have to operate your dried
fruit import business successfully.

1.  **Multi-Currency and Multi-Language Storefront:** The website must
    > feature explicit multi-currency pricing (specifically Euros for
    > the Netherlands) rather than simple conversion calculators, and
    > support content localization in Dutch and English 1-3.

2.  **Unified B2B and B2C Catalog Management:** The system must support
    > both Direct-to-Consumer (retail) and B2B (wholesale) customers
    > from a single dashboard, offering wholesale customer groups,
    > volume pricing, and customized invoicing 4, 5.

3.  **Supplier Portal and Ordering Integration:** The platform must
    > provide a secure portal or integration for your Philippine
    > suppliers or sourcing agents, allowing them to receive direct
    > purchase orders from you, update raw material statuses, and
    > facilitate seamless reordering to your Dutch storage facility 6-8.

4.  **Native Subscription Engine:** To capitalize on the snack box
    > trend, the website must have built-in subscription capabilities
    > allowing customers to set up flexible billing intervals (e.g.,
    > weekly or monthly deliveries of dried mangoes) without relying on
    > third-party apps 9-11.

5.  **Separate Billing and Fulfillment Scheduling:** The platform must
    > be able to charge customers on a subscription basis while
    > coordinating the logistics of international ingredient sourcing
    > and local Dutch delivery independently 9, 12, 13.

6.  **Automated Tax and Duty Calculation:** The checkout process must
    > integrate with tax compliance software (such as Avalara or TaxJar)
    > to automatically calculate regional VAT and import duties based on
    > the specific Harmonized System (HS) codes for dried fruits 3,
    > 14, 15.

7.  **Compliance and Traceability Tagging:** The product pages must
    > support custom attributes to accurately display mandatory EU Food
    > Law labeling, including ingredients, allergens, organic/\"clean
    > label\" certifications, country of origin, and supplier details 9,
    > 16, 17.

8.  **Multi-Location Inventory Tracking:** The system must track
    > inventory levels specifically at your Netherlands warehousing
    > facility, managing stock to prevent customers from ordering
    > out-of-stock or heavily clumped/expired fruit 13, 18, 19.

9.  **Automated Logistics Integrations:** The platform must connect with
    > express logistics providers (such as DHL or FedEx) to provide
    > real-time shipping rates, generate Air Waybills, and provide
    > shipment tracking for the end consumer 10, 20.

10. **Mixed Cart Support:** The checkout system must allow customers to
    > seamlessly combine one-time purchases (e.g., a single bag of
    > chili-seasoned mangoes) and recurring subscription bundles in a
    > single transaction 4, 9.

**Non-Functional Requirements (NFRs)**These define how the system must
perform, including its security, usability, and technical architecture.

1.  **System Scalability:** The website must utilize an API-first or
    > headless commerce architecture (like Swell) to ensure it can scale
    > operations from a startup to processing high volumes without
    > requiring enterprise-level IT overhauls or expensive platform
    > migrations 21, 22.

2.  **High Availability:** The e-commerce infrastructure must guarantee
    > at least a 99.99% uptime so that global suppliers, Dutch
    > customers, and wholesale distributors can access the platform
    > reliably 24/7 5, 23.

3.  **Regulatory Data Compliance:** The backend must reliably and
    > securely store mandatory import documentation---such as FDA
    > Licenses to Operate (LTO), Phytosanitary Certificates, and HACCP
    > compliance records---linked to the respective Philippine supplier
    > profiles for audit purposes 16, 24.

4.  **Cost Efficiency in Transactions:** The platform should feature a
    > zero-transaction-fee structure on external payment gateways to
    > preserve your profit margins as your import volume from the
    > Philippines scales 1, 5.

5.  **Payment Security:** The system must provide secure multi-gateway
    > payment processing that complies with international security
    > standards, allowing safe handling of credit cards for DTC sales
    > and secure invoicing for B2B wholesale buyers 9, 18.

6.  **Usability and Localization:** The user interface must be highly
    > intuitive, allowing seamless navigation for Dutch consumers while
    > ensuring that the checkout flow, product descriptions, and email
    > notifications match regional expectations 3.

7.  **System Maintainability:** To reduce long-term operational
    > complexity, the core e-commerce functions (like subscriptions and
    > wholesale tools) must be native to the platform, avoiding the
    > compatibility risks and maintenance costs associated with relying
    > on numerous third-party plugins 11.

8.  **Document Upload Reliability:** The supplier-facing features must
    > support the reliable uploading of critical clearance documents
    > (such as commercial invoices answering what the item is and its
    > use, packing lists, and pre-shipment lab reports) with sufficient
    > file size allowances 16, 25, 26.

9.  **Mobile Accessibility:** The storefront must be fully responsive
    > and optimized for mobile devices, enabling customers to easily
    > purchase products or manage their dried fruit subscriptions from
    > their smartphones 2, 22.

10. **Data Ownership and Portability:** While utilizing a hosted
    > solution for ease of use, the architecture must allow you to
    > retain control over your customer data, product catalogs, and
    > sales reports, ensuring you have clear visibility across the
    > entire import-to-sale business pipeline 18, 27.
