1. Executive Summary
--------------------

### Business Concept

A specialized import and distribution company focused on premium
Philippine dried tropical fruits (primarily dried mangoes, with
expansion into pineapple, papaya, and banana chips) targeting the Dutch
retail, wholesale, and direct-to-consumer markets.

### Value Proposition

-   Premium Philippine dried mangoes positioned as a "clean label,"
    healthy tropical snack
-   Full EU regulatory compliance with transparent traceability
-   Modern subscription-based e-commerce model for recurring revenue
-   Leverage the Netherlands' position as Europe's primary logistics
    gateway

### Target Markets

-   Direct-to-consumer (DTC) via localized Dutch webshop
-   B2B wholesale to Dutch/European health food retailers, supermarkets,
    and specialty stores
-   Export re-distribution hub for other EU countries

### Key Milestones

-   First commercial shipment: Month 7
-   Break-even: Month 14--16
-   First €1M revenue year: Year 3

2. Market Opportunity
---------------------

### The Netherlands as Strategic Entry Point

-   Europe's largest port (Rotterdam) and major re-export hub
-   Strong consumer demand for healthy, exotic, convenient snacks
-   Growing preference for "clean label" products (no added sugar, no
    sulfites, organic)
-   High e-commerce adoption and willingness to pay for premium imported
    snacks

### Product-Market Fit

Philippine dried mangoes are already well-regarded for quality and
taste. Consumer trends in 2025--2026 show strong alignment with: -
Clean-label positioning - Organic certification demand - Exotic flavor
innovations (e.g., chili-seasoned mango) - Subscription snack box models

### Competitive Positioning

Differentiate on: - Traceability and Philippine-origin storytelling -
Strict adherence to EU maximum residue limits and labeling rules -
Subscription convenience via modern headless commerce - Premium
positioning vs. generic Asian imports

3. Regulatory & Compliance Framework
------------------------------------

### Philippine Side Requirements

  Requirement                                     Description                               Priority
  ----------------------------------------------- ----------------------------------------- --------------------
  DTI Business Name Registration                  Required for sole proprietorships         Mandatory
  FDA License to Operate (LTO)                    Non-negotiable for food manufacturers     Mandatory
  FDA Certificate of Product Registration (CPR)   Product-specific safety compliance        Mandatory
  Client Profile Registration System (CPRS)       Bureau of Customs exporter registration   Mandatory
  HACCP Certification                             Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points   Highly Recommended
  GMP Certification                               Good Manufacturing Practice               Recommended
  Halal Certification                             Cultural alignment for EU buyers          Recommended

### EU / Dutch Import Requirements

-   **Phytosanitary Certificate** from Philippine Bureau of Plant
    Industry (BPI)
-   Compliance with EU maximum pesticide residue levels
-   Accurate labeling: ingredients, allergens, country of origin, net
    weight, supplier details, batch/lot codes
-   HS Code classification for dried mangoes (typically 0804.50 or
    related dried fruit codes)
-   Import declaration and payment of applicable duties + 21% Dutch VAT

### Critical Success Factor

Precise commodity code classification and complete documentation are the
primary defense against customs delays. Misclassification is the leading
cause of product spoilage and unexpected costs.

4. Supply Chain & Sourcing Strategy
-----------------------------------

### Supplier Selection Criteria

-   Registered CPRS exporters with active FDA LTO + CPR
-   Demonstrated HACCP compliance and traceability systems
-   Ability to provide pre-shipment samples + third-party lab testing
    (moisture, SO₂, contaminants, microbiology)
-   Consistent quality and scalable production capacity

### Packaging & Quality Preservation

-   Moisture-proof, food-grade films with desiccants (silica gel packs)
-   Tight shrink-wrapping of pallets to prevent dust and tampering
-   Clear labeling of production date, lot number, and "best before"

### Logistics Options

  Method                         Transit Time   Use Case                              Cost Profile
  ------------------------------ -------------- ------------------------------------- -----------------
  Sea Freight (20ft container)   4--6 weeks     Primary volume shipments              Most economical
  Air Freight                    3--5 days      Urgent replenishment, organic lines   Premium pricing

---------------------------------------

  Feature                           Benefit for This Business
  --------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------
  Native subscriptions              Recurring mango/snack boxes (monthly/quarterly)
  230+ currencies + 170 languages   Full Dutch localization + Euro pricing
  Separate billing & fulfillment    Charge now, ship when inventory arrives
  Unlimited product variants        Multiple sizes, organic vs conventional, spice levels
  Low TCO                           No transaction fees on external gateways

### Sales Channels

1.  **Primary:** Localized Dutch Swell storefront (DTC + subscription)
2.  **Secondary:** B2B wholesale portal (trade accounts with volume
    pricing)
3.  **Future:** Expansion to Germany, Belgium, and other EU markets

8. Risk Mitigation
------------------

  Risk Factor                        Mitigation Strategy
  ---------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Customs delays / rejection         Pre-verified HS codes, complete documentation, experienced Dutch customs broker
  Quality inconsistency              Mandatory third-party lab testing on every batch
  Currency fluctuation               Price in Euros; use Swell's multi-currency capabilities
  Spoilage / humidity damage         Strict moisture-proof packaging + desiccants
  Regulatory changes                 Close relationship with Dutch customs specialists and Philippine FDA
  Competition from cheaper imports   Premium positioning + traceability + subscription convenience

9. Phased Implementation Roadmap
--------------------------------

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1--2)

-   Register Dutch legal entity
-   Identify and audit 2--3 Philippine suppliers (CPRS, LTO, CPR, lab
    results)
-   Secure all required Philippine export certifications

### Phase 2: Regulatory & Product Readiness (Months 3--4)

-   Complete EU labeling compliance and packaging design
-   Register with Dutch Chamber of Commerce & obtain EORI number
-   Finalize Swell platform setup and Dutch localization

### Phase 3: First Import & Validation (Months 5--6)

-   Place first trial order (1×20ft container)
-   Full customs clearance process in Rotterdam
-   Conduct quality verification upon arrival

### Phase 4: Market Launch (Month 7)

-   Launch Dutch e-commerce store
-   Activate subscription options
-   Begin B2B outreach to health food retailers and wholesalers

### Phase 5: Scale & Optimize (Months 8--18)

-   Achieve consistent monthly container volumes
-   Expand product range (organic line, flavored variants)
-   Explore re-export opportunities to Germany and Belgium
-   Target €1M+ annual revenue by end of Year 3
